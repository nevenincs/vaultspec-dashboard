# `dashboard-node-canvas` adr: `node canvas browser` | (**status:** `accepted`)

## Problem Statement

The node canvas is the product's centerpiece — the graph is the product. It is the GPU-rendered field of the engine's `LinkageGraph`: feature and document node species, the four provenance tiers shown as line treatment, and the meta-edge ribbons that summarize a constellation. Everything else in the dashboard — the camera/nav toolbar, the data and lens controls, the minimap, the timeline, the inspector — exists to steer, filter, or annotate this one surface. The dashboard is now entering a design-driven recodification: the base design language has been re-pinned (trend-following agentic-desktop register, tier-as-treatment with grayscale-safe identity, warmth at the token layer only) and the iconography retired the hand-drawn glyph family in favor of Phosphor domain marks served through the existing texture seam.

This ADR recodifies the canvas itself — its node and edge visual encoding, its level-of-detail behaviour, and the browse interaction (constellation overview, hover ego-lift, click-select, open-in-place, expand-ego) — under the re-pinned language. It is spec work: it pins what the canvas renders and how it is browsed; it does not plan or perform the migration and changes no application code. The camera/nav toolbar (`dashboard-nav-controls`), the data and lens controls — tier dial, filters, working set, layout, discover (`dashboard-canvas-controls`) — and the minimap (`dashboard-minimap`) are deliberately out of scope; they are sibling ADRs that this one references for their seam but does not specify. The scope fence here is the rendered field and the browsing gestures over it, plus the DOM overlay islands and node-interior for opened nodes.

## Considerations

The canvas already exists in a mature, seam-disciplined form, and this recodification inherits rather than rebuilds it. The renderer side lives behind the `SceneController` seam in `frontend/src/scene/`: `sceneController.ts` defines the locked command and event union (`SceneCommand`, `SceneEvent`, `SceneAnchor`); the field assembly mounts a PixiJS v8 renderer; `field/nodeSprites.ts` carries node anatomy (silhouette sprite, progress-ring arc, lifecycle-tinted fill, freshness halo alpha, tier degree badges) with a far/near LOD switch; `field/edgeMeshes.ts` renders the four tiers as four line treatments plus the meta-edge ribbon and the semantic haze; `field/egoHighlight.ts` computes the hovered node's 1-hop set with a `RECEDE_ALPHA` dim of the rest; `field/camera.ts` owns the world transform and the three semantic-zoom levels (constellation, feature, document). The chrome side is the dumb view: `app/stage/Stage.tsx` mounts the field once per app lifetime, feeds it constellation keyframes and working-set ego expansions through stores hooks, and wires seam events back into shared selection; `app/islands/IslandLayer.tsx` floats DOM islands anchored to node screen positions via the seam's `trackNode`; `app/islands/NodeInterior.tsx` unfolds an opened node's canonical interior (the feature lifecycle axis, the plan's tiered steps).

The decisive inherited seam is the `GlyphTextureProvider` in `nodeSprites.ts`, implemented today by `ProgrammaticGlyphs` in `field/glyphs.ts`: it yields one white silhouette texture per node kind, generated on the GPU and tinted by the sprite layer. The iconography ADR fixed that the Phosphor domain marks (and the in-family-authored tier, lifecycle, node-feature, and progress marks) become canvas textures through exactly this seam — a texture swap behind the same provider interface, not a rendering change. This ADR treats that seam as the single insertion point for the new mark family and re-decides nothing about which marks are used or authored.

The base language requires of the canvas: tier-as-treatment with grayscale-safe identity (shape and line treatment first, hue as redundant reinforcement); structure felt not seen (the field leads, chrome attenuates); diff legibility as sacred (add/remove keep high-contrast green/red even in a warm theme); motion that is fast, subtle, and state-communicating, with keyboard actions instant and `prefers-reduced-motion` honored; tabular numerals on data-bearing labels and monospace reserved for true identity. The wire contract supplies the data: stable node and edge ids (the canvas caches and animates by id), the constellation-vs-document granularity split with engine-aggregated meta-edges, the bounded-query node ceiling with an honest `truncated` block, the per-tier `tiers` degradation block on every response, and the single delta clock that lets a held keyframe splice live deltas.

## Constraints

- **GPU is render-only; graph compute is CPU, in the engine.** The canvas computes no graph structure — no construction, diffing, projection, or query happens on the GPU or in the scene layer. Layout (ForceAtlas2) runs in a scene worker on the CPU over engine-served nodes; the GPU draws sprites, meshes, and textures. The engine holds no layout coordinates, so any spatial concern (viewport culling, fit-to-view) is client-side geometry over scene-owned positions, never a request for the engine to lay out the field.
- **Every graph read is bounded by default.** The canvas's default view is the constellation (feature) LOD — feature nodes plus meta-edge ribbons, normally bounded by feature count. Descent to document granularity is scoped (a feature filter or an ego/neighbor expansion), served under the engine's hard node ceiling, and the canvas must render the `truncated` block honestly as a "narrow your view" state rather than presenting a partial graph as complete. The canvas never requests, and must never expect, an unbounded full-document slice.
- **Stable ids drive object constancy.** Every sprite, mesh, island, and animation is keyed by the engine's stable node/edge id. The sprite layer reconciles by id (add/keep/remove), the island layer anchors by id, and animated transitions (add fades in, remove fades out, re-tier is a staged transition) preserve constancy because the id does not move. The canvas never derives its own identity for a node or edge and never re-keys on a resolution-state flip.
- **The scene receives data only via `SceneController` commands and emits selection/hover back via its event channel.** Data, visibility membership, time, pins, and deltas arrive as `SceneCommand`s; interaction leaves as `SceneEvent`s (hover, select, open, expand, pin). The scene never calls `fetch`, never reads the raw `tiers` block, and never holds wire state — the stores layer is the sole wire client and feeds the scene through the seam. The chrome (islands, interiors) is equally dumb: it consumes stores hooks and the seam's anchor subscription, never the engine directly.
- **What the canvas must NOT do.** It must not grow its own endpoint or fetch; must not define a node shape divergent from the contract's; must not encode categorical identity in hue alone (the grayscale projection must stand); must not animate keyboard-initiated actions; must not silently re-bucket an unknown tier (an unknown tier is a surfaced data error, matching the truthfulness stance); and must not become a second model — it is a view projecting over the one `LinkageGraph` mirrored in the stores.

## Implementation

The canvas is recodified as a rendering surface and a browse interaction, both inheriting the existing seam, the base tokens, and the Phosphor mark family. Concrete token and motion values are produced during adoption from the shared `:root` layer (read into the scene via `getComputedStyle`, the cross-layer constraint the base language pins); this ADR states the laws.

**Node and edge visual encoding.** Node type is carried by silhouette — one distinct mark per species (the doc-type set, the feature-convergence mark, the code-artifact mark) — sourced as Phosphor textures through the `GlyphTextureProvider`, replacing the programmatic placeholder polygons. Shape carries type; color is reserved for state, and the two channels never compete. Node anatomy layers, from the silhouette outward: a lifecycle fill (the sprite tint, resolved from state tokens), a progress ring (a parametric arc primitive, not an icon, drawn for plan and feature nodes from `done/total`), a freshness halo (alpha decaying from a recent modification toward a floor over the freshness window), tier degree badges (a compact per-tier count line, only populated tiers shown), and the label. Feature-convergence nodes are the constellation's centers of gravity: their radius grows log-scaled with member count so a large feature reads as an anchor without swamping the field; every other species keeps the base radius, since shape carries their type, not size.

**Amendment (graph-representation, 2026-06-14): salience drives size and label priority; it supersedes the member-count radius rule.** The salience and representation trio (`graph-node-salience-adr`, `graph-node-semantics-adr`, `graph-representation-adr`) was authored after this ADR and introduces a per-lens `salience` node field (an engine-computed, viewer-intent importance scalar) that this canvas now consumes. Two encodings on this surface change as a result, named here so an executor does not invent them:

- **`salience` drives node size for every species, and SUPERSEDES the member-count radius rule above.** When a node carries a `salience` value, its radius is a monotonic function of salience (making the importance field visible, per the representation encoding map), not of its species or member count. The prior rule — feature-convergence radius from member count, every other species at base radius — becomes the honest FALLBACK used only when salience is absent (an origin that does not yet serve it). The member-count signal does not compete with salience: a feature node's member count folds into its `salience` upstream (the salience ADR aggregates exec children and structural role into the field), so the two channels no longer collide. Size is therefore one channel (importance), reserved as before from the type channel (shape).
- **`salience` is a label-priority input to the DOI label cull.** The label culling described under "Level of detail" already prioritizes focused, pinned, and ego-lifted nodes; it now additionally orders the AMBIENT field by `salience` so the most important nodes label first as the field declutters — the DOI a-priori-importance term made visible in the label channel, not only the size channel.

Both encodings inherit the existing seam: the scene reads `salience` as a node field through the stores layer (never computing it), and the size/label values resolve from the same token-and-LOD discipline. No new channel competes with shape-for-type or color-for-state; salience occupies the size and label-priority channels the encoding map assigns it.

Edges encode the four provenance tiers as four fixed line treatments — declared as a solid inked line, structural as a solid status-colored line (resolved/stale/broken), temporal as a dotted line, semantic as a soft light haze whose width tracks score. Line treatment is the primary channel and hue is secondary, so the encoding reads in pure grayscale; confidence rides lightness (a mix toward the canvas ground), never transparency alone, preserving the channel-separation the base language and the prior GUI ADR require. Edge direction is rendered from the contract's `src`-to-`dst` ordering as arrowheads that appear at document LOD. At constellation granularity the inter-feature relationships render as engine-aggregated meta-edge ribbons — a quad whose thickness is the aggregated count, the per-tier breakdown unfolding on hover — never as client-flattened document edges; the canvas consumes the separate meta-edge array the engine serves and never synthesizes it. Diff coloring (added/removed during timeline replay) keeps its high-contrast semantics overriding warmth.

**Level of detail.** The constellation (feature) LOD is the default, unbounded-safe view; descent to the document graph is a scoped, ceiling-bounded query. LOD is two-fold: the camera's discrete semantic levels (constellation, feature, document) gate what unfolds, and the sprite layer's far/near switch governs anatomy cost — the far field draws silhouette and state fill only, while full anatomy (ring, badges, label) renders above the near-zoom threshold and always for focused or lifted nodes. Labels follow a degree-of-interest culling rather than a hard zoom gate: focused, pinned, and ego-lifted nodes show labels at any zoom, while the ambient field shows labels only near the threshold, so the overview never becomes a hairball of text. Arrowheads and document-level edge detail appear at document LOD.

**The browse interaction.** Browsing is the cohort's progressive-disclosure law applied to the graph: overview first, zoom and filter, details on demand. The default is the scoped constellation. Hovering a node lifts its 1-hop ego (the node, its neighbors, and the incident edges keep full treatment and labels) while the rest of the field recedes to the dim receded alpha — focus is highlight-neighbors plus dim-the-rest, never hide. A single click selects, emitting the one shared selection that every region honors (selecting on the canvas focuses everywhere, and a selection from another region flows back as a focus command). Opening a node (double-click or keyboard) unfolds it in place as a DOM island anchored to its screen position, with the node interior rendered as a reviewable, scannable document in the cohort's instrument grammar — a feature unfolds into its canonical lifecycle axis (research, adr, plan, exec, audit), a plan into its tiered steps with check state, each entry clickable into the shared selection. Expanding a node's ego (the distinct expand gesture) materializes its neighbor subgraph on the stage as a bounded ego query and adds it to the explicit, visible working set, so the field grows by deliberate descent rather than by dumping the whole graph. Pins fix a node's layout position and force its label on. The island scales with the camera but clamps to a readable range, and islands are the only pointer-interactive region of the overlay.

**States.** The canvas renders every wire condition as a designed state, never as a raw error. Loading shows a scope-appropriate message (loading the constellation, loading the document graph, or waiting for a worktree scope). The empty/no-graph state is an invitation, not a void: a worktree with no vault corpus reads as "this worktree has no second brain yet" with the next step, in the approachable copy tone the base language sanctions. Per-tier degradation from the `tiers` block renders the affected tier as an honestly-absent state (a missing semantic tier draws no semantic haze and says so where surfaced), never as a failure of the whole canvas. A truncated bounded query renders the capped subgraph plus an honest "narrowed — refine your view" affordance derived from the `truncated` block. An unknown tier on the wire is surfaced as a data error rather than silently re-bucketed. An interior or detail fetch failure renders a contained "interior unavailable" state on that island, not a canvas-wide error.

**Keyboard operability, a11y, and motion.** The canvas is keyboard-operable: a focused node can be moved across the graph by walking its edges (arrow/Tab over the ego set), select/open/expand have keyboard equivalents, and keyboard-initiated camera and selection actions are instant — they do not animate, per the base motion law. Identity never rests on hue: the tier line treatments and the node silhouettes are distinguishable in grayscale at the legibility floor, and the degradation and selection states carry a non-color cue (treatment, fill, ring) so the canvas is usable without color perception. `prefers-reduced-motion` swaps the ego-lift, visibility-fade, and diff-replay transitions for instant state changes. Where the canvas does animate a data change (live deltas, timeline replay), it obeys the established animated-transitions grammar — add fades in, remove fades out, re-tier is a distinct staged transition, the same semantic operation always looks the same, and states that share no structure are cut, not tweened — with object constancy guaranteed by stable ids.

**Layer ownership and icon textures.** The scene is the face: it consumes `SceneController` commands fed by the stores layer (the sole wire client), emits select/hover/open/expand/pin events, and renders. It never fetches, never reads the raw `tiers` block, and never defines a node shape of its own — it projects over the one `LinkageGraph` mirrored in the stores. The chrome islands and interiors are the glass: they read stores hooks and the seam's anchor subscription and emit intent back, never touching the engine. Icon textures enter through the iconography ADR's seam: the Phosphor domain marks and the in-family-authored tier/state/node-feature/ring marks are turned into textures behind the unchanged `GlyphTextureProvider`, so adopting the new family is a provider implementation swap, not a change to the sprite, edge, or browse code. All visual values — surfaces, tier hues, state colors, motion durations, density, radius — come from the shared token layer applying the base language; the canvas hard-codes none of them.

## Rationale

The recodification is almost entirely an inheritance, and that is the point. The four-layer ownership and the locked `SceneController` seam already settle who renders the graph and how data reaches it; the prior GUI cycle already built tier-as-treatment, the ego-lift focus model, semantic-zoom LOD, the meta-edge ribbon, and open-in-place islands; the graph-scale-hardening cycle already bounded every read; and the constellation-live-delta work already gave the canvas a single delta clock for gapless live animation. The base design-language and iconography ADRs supply the only genuinely new inputs — a re-pinned token register and a retired-then-replaced mark family — and both land at insertion points the canvas already has: the shared `:root` tokens it reads via `getComputedStyle`, and the `GlyphTextureProvider` seam the iconography ADR explicitly chose as the mark-to-texture bridge. Pinning the canvas as a projection over the one model, encoded by treatment-first grayscale-safe channels and browsed by progressive disclosure, lets this ADR recodify the product's centerpiece without reopening its architecture, its wire contract, or its seam — exactly the discipline the existing rules require.

The treatment-first, hue-redundant encoding is retained because it is both the accessibility floor (grayscale-distinguishable identity) and the product's headline reading of provenance; the base language re-affirms it and the OKLCH tier ramp makes it correct by construction. The browse model — constellation overview, hover ego-lift, click-select, open-in-place, expand-ego, with focus as highlight-plus-dim — is retained because it is the converged agentic-desktop progressive-disclosure grammar applied to a graph and was validated in the prior cycle; this ADR codifies it as the canvas's law rather than inventing a new interaction.

## Consequences

- **Gains.** The product's centerpiece is recodified under the new language with near-zero architectural churn: the mark family changes through one seam, the palette and motion change through the shared token layer, and the browse and LOD laws are made explicit so a future agent inherits them. The grayscale-safe, bounded, stable-id, layer-owned canvas stays correct across themes, scales, and live updates by construction.
- **Costs and difficulties.** The Phosphor-to-texture path must be proven against the `GlyphTextureProvider` seam during adoption (the iconography ADR flags this risk), and each domain mark must re-pass the 14px grayscale-by-shape gate as a canvas texture, not only as a chrome icon. Tier line treatments and state fills must be re-validated for grayscale separation and contrast under the warm theme, since the canvas ground shifts effective contrast. Keyboard graph-walk and reduced-motion paths are operability surfaces that are easy to under-build and must be exercised explicitly.
- **Risks.** Warmth or decorative motion could creep into the field and erode the instrument register the base language guards; the canvas is the largest surface and the most tempting place to over-animate. A future view might be tempted to fetch the document graph unbounded for "completeness" — the bounded-default and truncation-honesty laws must hold. The mark swap could regress the grayscale identity if a Phosphor silhouette collides with another at small size.
- **Pathways opened.** A clean token-and-texture inheritance makes future canvas mark additions cheap; the explicit browse and LOD laws give the sibling control ADRs (nav, lens, minimap) a stable surface to steer; and the projection-over-one-model stance keeps any future canvas-adjacent view (a table or alternate projection) consistent for free.

## Codification candidates

None. The constraints this ADR honors are already codified — `graph-compute-is-cpu-gpu-is-render-and-search`, `graph-queries-are-bounded-by-default`, `dashboard-layer-ownership`, `views-are-projections-of-one-model`, and `provenance-stable-keys-are-identity-bearing` — and the new visual obligations (treatment-first grayscale identity, warmth-in-tokens, icons from the two sanctioned families) are pending candidates on the base design-language and iconography ADRs that must hold across a full execution cycle before promotion. This ADR introduces no further durable cross-surface constraint of its own.
