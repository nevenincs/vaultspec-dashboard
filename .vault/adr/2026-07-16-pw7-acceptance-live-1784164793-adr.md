# `pw7-acceptance-live-1784164793` adr: SSE reconnection and cursor-persistence contract for long-lived dashboard event streams | (**status:** `accepted`)

## Problem Statement

Long-lived dashboard SSE streams drop for ordinary reasons — network blips, engine restarts, the 60 s hidden-tab pause, slow-consumer lag — and the acceptance-live harness (`pw7-acceptance-live-1784164793`) needs a frozen reconnection-and-resume contract to assert against. As `2026-07-16-pw7-acceptance-live-1784164793-research` establishes, a stream that silently drops deltas leaves the graph, tree, and lifecycle panels stale with no operator signal, and a resume that replays against a reset clock splices mixed-generation state (the load-time artefact). The research found the dashboard has already converged on two distinct strategies rather than one, split by a single axis — whether a missed event is re-derivable from an authoritative snapshot or is durable truth that would otherwise be lost. This ADR ratifies that split as the governing contract, fixes the resiliency floor every long-lived stream must honour, and closes the open question of whether the resume cursor should gain durable client-side persistence.

## Considerations

The decisive factor, per `2026-07-16-pw7-acceptance-live-1784164793-research`, is **event replaceability**: re-derivable channels (`graph`, `backends`, `git`, `fs`, `index`) can always recover by re-keyframing from an authoritative snapshot, so their resume cursor may be ephemeral and best-effort; irreplaceable authoring lifecycle events (approvals, apply receipts) cannot be re-derived and so demand exact, durable replay. The research grounds that both models already exist in production code and share a monotonic-`seq` frame shape while differing in persistence and recovery. Also weighed: consistency with the governing hidden-tab-pause precedent (`2026-06-13-dashboard-live-state-adr`, D4), which treats a deliberate reconnect gap on an unanchored channel as a re-snapshot rather than degradation; the durable-outbox precedent (`2026-06-29-agentic-streaming-events-outbox-adr`); and the delta-resume contracts of `2026-07-13-graph-slice-delta-adr` and `2026-07-12-vault-tree-delta-adr`. The acceptance harness must be able to observe both recovery paths — re-keyframe on gap, and exact replay on the durable stream.

## Considered options

- **A — Ephemeral in-memory ring + `since=` resume for re-derivable channels (chosen, in combination with B).** Cheap, fully bounded, and correct because a gap or restart always falls back to an authoritative snapshot. Kept as the strategy for every re-derivable channel; rejected as the *sole* strategy because it cannot carry irreplaceable lifecycle truth a gap would silently drop.
- **B — Durable transactional outbox + exact `last_seq` cursor for lifecycle truth (chosen, in combination with A).** Survives engine restart, replays exactly within a bounded window, degrades to a recovery snapshot beyond it. Kept for irreplaceable events; rejected as the *universal* strategy because a durable outbox per graph delta is unwarranted cost for state cheaper to re-derive than to replay.
- **C — Native `EventSource` + `Last-Event-ID` auto-reconnect.** Works on the wire since the engine already emits `.id(seq)`, but rejected: it yields no multiplexed-channel control, no TanStack cache integration, no `AbortController`-driven pause/cancel, and no query-param cursor that folds into the cache key — and `Last-Event-ID` alone still cannot express gap detection or backward-reset re-keyframe, which are application logic regardless of transport.
- **D — Persist the ephemeral cursor to `sessionStorage`/`localStorage` for cross-reload resume.** Rejected: a persisted cursor is stale the instant the ring evicts it or the engine restarts (clock reset), both already handled by re-keyframe, so it adds a stale-cursor branch guarding only a snapshot fetch that is already fast and rate-floored.

## Constraints

All parent mechanisms this decision rests on are mature and already in production, so there is no frontier-technology risk. The ephemeral model is bounded at every accumulator the research pinned: the engine resume ring, the smaller broadcast channel capacity, the per-cell atomic `seq`, the client-side retention array with seq-dedup, and the paired `gcTime` that reclaims an unobserved stream — consistent with the project's resource-bounds law. The durable model is bounded by a finite replay page cap that degrades to a recovery snapshot on `cursor_ahead_of_high_water` and `replay_window_exceeded` rather than replaying unboundedly. The binding constraint on the harness itself is observability, flagged as uninvestigated in the research: deterministically forcing a ring-eviction gap requires accumulating more frames than the ring holds while a consumer is detached, and forcing a broadcast `Lagged` requires exceeding the broadcast capacity un-consumed. Keep-alive interval interaction with intermediary proxy buffering and mobile background-throttling beyond the 60 s hidden pause are likewise uninvestigated and out of scope for this contract.

## Implementation

The two-model split is ratified as the standing contract; no new transport or cursor-persistence mechanism is introduced. Re-derivable channels keep the ephemeral path: a per-scope-cell broadcast subscription resumed from an in-memory ring via a `since=` query param, consumed on the client through a `fetch` + `ReadableStream` reader that folds the cursor into the TanStack Query key, caps oversized frames, retains a bounded seq-deduped window, and reconnects with capped exponential backoff. The resiliency floor is fixed as three mandatory behaviours the research grounds: an explicit `gap` event when the needed seq predates the oldest buffered frame or a slow consumer lags (never a silent drop), subscribe-before-snapshot ordering so a rebuild between the two is queued not lost, and the client re-keyframe guards — an empty resume read as an engine restart and a backward seq read as a clock reset whose partial batch is discarded — with fallback re-drains rate-floored. Irreplaceable authoring lifecycle events keep the durable path: a transactional outbox with a persisted monotonic seq, resumed by `last_seq` and, on cold start, a recovery route returning an authoritative snapshot plus the `next_seq` to continue from, with finite bounded replay degrading honestly past its window. The client-held cursor stays in-memory in both models; durability lives server-side, and a page reload re-syncs from an authoritative snapshot. The acceptance-live harness asserts, per model, that a forced gap re-keyframes rather than splicing stale state and that the durable stream replays exactly within its window and recovers to a snapshot beyond it, as scoped by `2026-07-16-pw7-acceptance-live-1784164793-research`.

## Rationale

The research evidence favours preserving the two-model split keyed on event replaceability over collapsing to one strategy, and over adding durable client-side cursor persistence. Option A alone cannot protect irreplaceable lifecycle truth, and option B universally imposes durable-outbox cost on state that is cheaper to re-derive — so the replaceability axis is exactly the right discriminator, and both models already exist and are proven in the code the research cites. Option C is refused because gap detection and backward-reset re-keyframe are application concerns that a raw `Last-Event-ID` transport cannot express, and swapping to `EventSource` would forfeit the multiplexing, cache integration, and pause/cancel control the current fetch-stream client provides. Option D is refused because every case a persisted cursor would help — a small reconnect gap against a fast, rate-floored snapshot — is already handled by re-keyframe, while every case it would hurt — ring eviction, engine restart clock reset — makes the persisted cursor stale and adds a branch guarding nothing worth guarding. Ratifying the split also keeps the contract consistent with the hidden-tab-pause precedent in `2026-06-13-dashboard-live-state-adr` and the durable-outbox precedent in `2026-06-29-agentic-streaming-events-outbox-adr`.

## Consequences

Gains: one clear rule — replaceable versus durable — tells any future channel author which contract to adopt; the resiliency floor (gap-on-eviction, gap-on-lag, subscribe-before-snapshot, empty-and-backward re-keyframe guards) becomes an assertable acceptance surface rather than tribal knowledge; and both paths stay fully bounded, so no reconnection strategy can leak an unbounded accumulator. Difficulties: the harness must engineer adversarial conditions the normal system avoids — over-filling the ring while detached to force eviction, and exceeding broadcast capacity un-consumed to force `Lagged` — which the research flags as uninvestigated and which this decision makes a prerequisite of proving the re-keyframe paths. Opens: keep-alive-versus-proxy-buffering tuning and mobile background-throttling beyond the 60 s hidden pause remain open questions the harness does not close; and if a future channel emerges whose events are neither cheaply re-derivable nor already covered by the authoring outbox, the replaceability axis will need an explicit third ruling rather than a silent default. Supersession posture: this ADR ratifies and freezes the existing split for the acceptance-live feature; a later change to the resume-cursor persistence model or a transport swap would supersede it and must be reviewed as a deliberate contract event, never an incidental refactor.
